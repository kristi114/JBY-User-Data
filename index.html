03 PATTERNS ACROSS THE ENTIRE VISIT

Navigation Is
The #1 Action

The most-clicked element on the homepage is the mechanism that reveals where users can go next. Visitors are not deciding between the menu and other homepage features; they are opening the menu to find them.
526 clicks on the hamburger, nearly 4× any individual destination · 9 of the top 10 destinations are reachable from that menu.

Only Half the
Page Gets Seen

Average scroll depth is below the midpoint and drop-off starts early. The further an element sits from the top of the page, the fewer visitors encounter it, regardless of device.
48.3% avg scroll · drop-off begins at 20% of page height · avg fold ~925px desktop, ~846px tablet, ~702px mobile.

Events Earn Traffic
On Their Own

Event pages pull significant traffic despite minimal prominence on the current homepage. The Newport Beach Boat Show page alone outperformed About Us, Brands, and all but two individual boat listings.
495 sessions on the Newport Beach event page, the 3rd most-visited page on the entire site.

Inventory Dominates
User Intent

When visitors know what they want, they want boats. Inventory filter pages outpace every other content type by an order of magnitude, while brand, location, and service pages draw a much smaller share of attention.
/inventory/filter 1,452 · /brands 233 · /locations/newport-harbor 99 · /yacht-management 91.
